# Positioning

## Target Customer

**Primary:** Solo developers and small teams (1-5 people) who are building something with code but feel stuck.

Specifically:
- Has a GitHub repo (or multiple)
- Has written more than a trivial amount of code
- Feels uncertainty about architecture, priorities, or next steps
- Would rather pay $75-$150 for clarity than waste weeks going in circles
- Values practical advice over theoretical consulting

**Secondary:** Students and bootcamp graduates who have built a portfolio project but want it reviewed by someone who ships production code.

## Pain Points

1. **"I do not know if I am building this right."** -- Architecture paralysis, too many options, fear of making the wrong choice early.

2. **"I have a mess and do not know what to fix first."** -- Tech debt, no tests, no CI, no deployment pipeline. Everything feels urgent, nothing feels important.

3. **"I need a plan, not a lecture."** -- Most reviews give vague feedback ("add tests," "improve structure"). This delivers a prioritized, dated execution plan.

4. **"I cannot afford a $200/hour consultant but I cannot figure this out alone."** -- The gap between "free Stack Overflow advice" and "expensive consultant" is enormous. This fills it.

## Offer Positioning

**What it is:** A fixed-price, fixed-scope audit of your codebase with a practical plan you can execute.

**What it is not:** Consulting. Strategy. Hand-holding. A guarantee of success.

**The framing:** "Ship with a plan instead of guessing."

**Why someone buys:**
- They have been stuck long enough to know they need outside help
- The price is low enough that the opportunity cost of NOT buying exceeds the price
- They want something concrete (a report, a plan, a PR), not abstract advice
- They want it fast (48h-5 days), not scheduled for weeks out
- They do not want to hire someone full-time or go through a lengthy engagement process

## Differentiators

| Instead of... | We offer... |
|---|---|
| $200/hour consultants | Fixed-price audits starting at $75 |
| Vague code review feedback | Prioritized, actionable plans with dates |
| Months of back-and-forth | Delivery in 48h-5 days |
| Generic "best practices" advice | Specific to YOUR repo, YOUR stack, YOUR situation |
| Ongoing retainer required | Buy one audit, get one plan, decide next steps yourself |

## Messaging Principles

- Be specific about what is included and what is not
- Use plain language -- no startup jargon
- Lead with the problem, not the technology
- Show the deliverable (a real report snippet, not a screenshot of a dashboard)
- Emphasize speed and clarity over comprehensiveness
