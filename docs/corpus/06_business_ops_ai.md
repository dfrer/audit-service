# Business Operations AI -- Practical Usage Guide
## How Businesses Actually Use AI and Where It Fails

### Where AI Works Well in Business

#### Customer Support Triage
AI for initial classification and routing of support tickets. Not for handling the full interaction, but for categorizing and prioritizing.

- **Good for:** Ticket classification, urgency scoring, routing to right team
- **Bad for:** Full automated responses without human review
- **Red flag:** AI responding to customers with no human oversight

#### Content Generation Support
Brainstorming, drafting, and editing assistance. The human still owns the voice and final quality.

- **Good for:** First drafts, headline variations, SEO metadata, content ideation
- **Bad for:** Publishing AI output without review, brand voice without human guidance
- **Red flag:** AI-written blog posts that sound like AI

#### Research and Analysis
Processing large documents, summarizing reports, extracting insights from data.

- **Good for:** Summarizing long reports, comparing vendor options, market research synthesis
- **Bad for:** Factual research without verification, financial modeling without human review
- **Red flag:** Board decisions based solely on AI analysis

#### Internal Knowledge
Using AI to navigate internal documentation, onboarding, and FAQ answering.

- **Good for:** New hire onboarding, documentation search, process explanation
- **Bad for:** Stale knowledge without updates, no source attribution
- **Red flag:** AI giving outdated policy information

### Where AI Typically Fails in Business

#### The "AI Strategy" That Is Just a Tool List
Buying tools and calling it strategy. Real strategy starts with a problem, not a purchase.

#### No Usage Policy
No guidelines about what can and cannot be put into AI tools. Data handling blind spot.

#### Tool Proliferation Without Integration
Each department buys its own AI tool. Nothing is connected. Total cost is unknown.

#### Expectation Mismatch
Leadership expects AI to "replace" workers. Workers expect AI to "do everything." Both are wrong.

### Audit Checklist for Business AI Ops

1. List all AI subscriptions and their monthly cost
2. For each tool, identify: primary user, use case, utilization rate
3. Identify overlapping capabilities across tools
4. Check for AI usage policy (exists? documented? enforced?)
5. Check data handling: what data types flow through which tools?
6. Identify any critical business processes dependent on AI tools
7. Check fallback plans if AI tools become unavailable
8. Calculate AI spend as percentage of total tool budget
9. Identify unused or underused AI tools that can be cut
10. Document who is responsible for AI tool decisions
